# The Geometry of Ecological Interactions: Simplifying Spatial Complexity (Dieckmann, Law, Metz, eds., 2000)

Cambridge Studies in Adaptive Dynamics #1. 580 pages, 23 chapters across 5 parts. Mathematical/theoretical; heavier reading. Cite by chapter number + author(s). Grep `dieckmann_geometry_full_text.md` for exact passages.

**Stance:** The corpus's **spatial-structure and population-dynamics theorist.** Use this source when the claim under review turns on *why* mean-field/pairwise reasoning is or isn't adequate — neighborhood effects, density thresholds, spatial heterogeneity, invasion fitness, adaptive dynamics. Directly relevant to AgroEco's known gaps: "static role assignments for context-dependent organisms" and "species-pair claims extrapolated to community-level regulation."
